What location changes
St. Joe runs slightly cleaner for the direct salmon shot. St. Joseph tends to fit the sharper harbor-mouth king setup, while Saugatuck leans a little more weather-sensitive and town-friendly once the boat is done.
What the month changes
September pushes the classic staging window. As the season moves from July into October, sunrise shifts later, surface temperatures fall, and the fish move from scattered summer water toward tighter pre-spawn structure.
What time of day changes
Gray light is the easiest screenshot window. Before and just after sunrise, low-angle light keeps fish higher and more aggressive. Once the sun gets up, captains usually have to work deeper and troll more precisely.

Michigan trip rules
  • Anglers age 17 and up need a Michigan fishing license. The 2026 license year runs from April 1, 2026 through March 31, 2027.
  • Daily all-species license is $10 for residents or nonresidents, and the purchaser sets the 24-hour start time.
  • Annual nonresident all-species license is $76, plus the $1 DNR Sportcard fee listed by the state.
  • For Great Lakes trout and salmon, Lake Michigan is open all year and the general minimum size is 10 inches, but lake trout use a separate management-unit table.
  • If anyone plans to fish inland trout or salmon waters before or after the charter, the 2026 digest says to check the county exceptions and Michigan Fishing Maps because seasons and tackle rules can change by waterbody.
  • The 2026 regulations also clarified bead fishing for trout and salmon: a bead on the hook, or pegged no more than 4 inches above a single-pointed hook, counts as an artificial lure.
  • Most charter crews allow moderate beer or wine, but hard liquor is commonly prohibited.
